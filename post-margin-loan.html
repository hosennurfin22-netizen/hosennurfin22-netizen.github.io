BSEC eases margin loan restrictions — a liquidity boost that needs guardrails

July 2026 · 3 min read

The news: The BSEC has finalized a draft to ease several existing restrictions on margin loans. The most notable change withdraws the current rule blocking margin loans for "B" category shares — those paying under 5% dividend, where investors previously needed a minimum Tk5 lakh investment held for at least a year. The draft also allows investors to buy non-marginable shares with cash inside a margin account, and lifts the ban preventing homemakers and students from taking margin loans. Alongside this, the Commission approved DSE and CSE to launch Scrip Netting — letting investors buy and sell the same stock multiple times in a single day — though it will take at least four months to build the required software, starting with around 30 blue-chip stocks. Some market stakeholders have flagged concern that, given a prolonged IPO supply shortage, easier margin financing could fuel excess demand for the same shares and abnormal price hikes; BSEC has said the intent is to resolve operational complexities, not create artificial demand.

Source: Dhaka Tribune — read the full report

What this could mean for the market

The withdrawal of these restrictions is expected to improve market liquidity and increase trading activity, helping revive the current dry market. Greater flexibility in margin financing can encourage participation and make the Bangladesh capital market more dynamic.

Margin trading can amplify gains — but it magnifies losses just as easily when the market turns.

The risk side of the equation

However, margin trading also carries higher risks. While it can amplify gains, it can equally magnify losses when the market moves downward. Therefore, alongside this relaxation, BSEC must ensure robust market monitoring, effective risk management, and strict enforcement of regulations to safeguard investors' interests and maintain market stability.

If implemented prudently, these reforms can strike the right balance between market development and investor protection.

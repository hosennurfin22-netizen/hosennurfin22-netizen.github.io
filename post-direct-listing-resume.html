BSEC resumes direct listing — a bold reform that must avoid old mistakes

July 2026 · 3 min read

The news: BSEC Chairman Mr. Masud Khan announced that renowned multinational and large corporations will be brought to the stock market through direct listing, aiming to eliminate unnecessary delays for well-established firms. Direct listing allows the sale of existing shares without underwriters or the creation of new shares — a simpler route than a traditional IPO, and typically cheaper. The mechanism was in effect in Bangladesh between 2006 and 2015 but was discontinued after some companies misused it to get listed at inflated prices. Alongside this announcement, the BSEC chief also criticized market-development spending on roadshows, and said regular risk-based audits of brokerage houses are being introduced to improve transparency following past incidents of fund embezzlement by stock brokers.

Source: The Daily Star — read the full report

A bold, timely move

Resuming direct listing is a bold and timely initiative by the new BSEC commission. A simpler listing process, coupled with strong corporate governance, can encourage multinational corporations and other fundamentally strong companies to enter Bangladesh's capital market. This will broaden the pool of quality listed securities, enhance market depth, and increase investor participation.

The regulator must ensure the mistakes of the past are not repeated.

The history this reform has to answer to

However, the regulator must ensure that the mistakes of the past are not repeated. Direct listing was previously discontinued due to concerns over inflated valuations and unjustified share prices. Therefore, BSEC should establish a transparent and robust valuation framework, enforce strict due diligence, and maintain close regulatory oversight to prevent price manipulation and protect investors.

If implemented properly, reopening direct listing could be a landmark reform that strengthens the quality of listed companies, restores investor confidence, and supports the long-term development of Bangladesh's capital market.

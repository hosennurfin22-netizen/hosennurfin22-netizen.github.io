BSEC's digital authorisation system closes a loophole brokers exploited

July 2026 · 3 min read

The news: BSEC Chairman Mr. Masud Khan announced that the regulator is introducing a secure digital authorisation system for investors and tightening oversight of brokerage houses through regular risk-based audits. The core reform replaces manual investor signatures with a fully digital authorisation process, built through a new back-office platform under development at the Dhaka Stock Exchange. Under the new system, brokerage houses will no longer be able to alter an investor's mobile number, email, or other account information without the client's digital approval — closing a gap that has previously enabled unauthorised account manipulation and misappropriation of investor funds. Alongside this, BSEC has directed the DSE to convert its surveillance department into an AI-based monitoring system within a year, aimed at automatically detecting abnormal price movements and suspending trading in stocks that show manipulation patterns.

Source: The Financial Express — read the full report

Why this is overdue

The introduction of a digital authorization system is a commendable initiative by the BSEC to strengthen investor protection in Bangladesh's capital market. By replacing manual authorizations with a secure digital process, the risk of unauthorized alterations to BO account information by brokerage houses will be significantly reduced. This will enhance transparency, improve accountability, and build greater trust among investors.

Removing the human hand from account edits removes the easiest point of abuse.

Efficiency, not just protection

Moreover, digital authorization will make transactions faster, more efficient, and less time-consuming by minimizing paperwork and manual intervention. Combined with AI-based market surveillance and stronger oversight of brokerage firms, these reforms have the potential to curb fraudulent activities and market manipulation, ultimately contributing to a more transparent, secure, and investor-friendly capital market.

This is truly a praiseworthy and timely decision by the regulator.

Intraday trading arrives in Bangladesh — a reform that rewards preparation

July 2026 · 3 min read

The news: The Bangladesh Securities and Exchange Commission has approved intraday trading, known as Scrip Netting, for the country's stock market. The mechanism will let investors buy and sell the same stock within a single trading session, without waiting for the normal settlement cycle. It's designed to modernize the market, improve liquidity, increase trading efficiency, and bring Bangladesh closer to international norms. The facility will initially be introduced for fundamentally strong listed companies, once the necessary technical and operational arrangements are in place.

Why this is a landmark move

The introduction of intraday trading is a landmark decision by the new BSEC Commission and one of the most significant reforms in Bangladesh's capital market in recent years. It will make the market more competitive and bring it closer to the practices followed in developed and emerging international capital markets. By increasing liquidity and trading flexibility, it has the potential to attract more institutional and foreign investors while boosting overall market confidence.

Intraday trading rewards preparation, not speculation.

The responsibility that comes with it

This opportunity comes with greater responsibility. Knowledgeable and disciplined investors can benefit from short-term trading opportunities, but inexperienced or uninformed investors may incur substantial losses if they trade impulsively without proper analysis and risk management.

Overall, this reform is a positive step toward building a more efficient, dynamic, and globally competitive capital market. If implemented with robust surveillance, investor education, and effective risk controls, it can significantly enhance investor confidence and contribute to the long-term development of Bangladesh's capital market.
